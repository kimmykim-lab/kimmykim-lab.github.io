📚 Courses

LIS 520 — Concepts, Services, and Issues for Information Professionals

Explores role of information professionals in mediating between individuals and resources in an ever‑changing information environment; including information production, distribution, selection, organization, and services to facilitate access to diverse users.

LIS 564 — Multicultural Resources for Youth

Facilitates development of cross‑cultural competence through authentic resources for children, tweens, and teens produced by or about ethnic minorities in the U.S. “Issues” focus: critical examination of genres and strategies to use them.

LIS 570 — Research, Assessment, and Design

Students recognize research and design opportunities, translate them into researchable frameworks, and conduct research in libraries and other information agencies: problem definition, data collection/analysis, design/validation of solutions, and reporting.

LIS 510 — Information Behavior

Introduction to user‑centered information behavior with theoretical and social‑justice foundations: information need, creation, seeking/sharing, assessment, management, and use. Includes synthesis of studies, fieldwork, and design implications.

LIS 530 — Organization of Information and Resources

Issues in organizing information: document characteristics; surrogate creation (cataloging standards, metadata element selection); classification and indexing languages; controlled vocabularies; and ethical implications of knowledge organization.

LIS 543 — Relational Database Management Systems

Relational database design, implementation, and management: data modeling, conceptual & logical design, physical implementation, SQL, data management, and DBA practices.

LIS 511 — Introduction to Programming for Information and Data Science

Foundations of programming for data science: syntax, data structures, functions, command‑line environments, version control; emphasizes algorithmic thinking, abstraction, debugging, and code reuse. No prior programming required.

LIS 580 — Management of Information Organizations

Internal/external management issues in information organizations: org behavior & theory, talent management, budgeting, service/operational planning, decision making, evaluation; plus environments, advocacy, strategic planning, communication, funding.

IMT 589 — Cloud Computing and AI: Tools, Services, and Applications

Introduces cloud computing with focus on AI applications: SaaS/PaaS/IaaS for ML/AI; evaluation by cost, security, compliance, fault tolerance, DR; use of leading providers’ tools and pre‑built APIs (e.g., LLMs, speech/vision/language) to deploy intelligent apps.

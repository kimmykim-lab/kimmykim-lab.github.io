How Teens Co-designed Mental Health Support in Public Libraries

Currently ongoing (Summer–Fall 2025)

Summary: In response to the teen mental health crisis, this ongoing project explores how public libraries can serve as supportive community hubs through the Nourishing Minds program. Across nine U.S. libraries, 29 trained staff are working with 119 teens to co-design programs, resources, and wellness spaces that reflect youth needs and priorities. Early outcomes suggest that participatory design enhances teens’ emotional well-being, social connection, and sense of agency—reimagining what mental health support can look like in library settings.

Skills & Tools: Participatory Design, Mixed Methods Research, Program Facilitation, Wellness Resource Design, Coding (Open, Field Note, Survey Data, Artificial Data)

Topics & Methods: Teen Mental Health, Library-based Support, Youth Empowerment, Co-creation Workshops, Case Study Analysis

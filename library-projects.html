Social Information Behavior Model

Summary: Developed a conceptual model to examine how trust, anonymity, and moderation influence information sharing and verification in online communities. The project draws from Dervins Sense-Making Theory and Bourdieus Social Capital Theory to analyze behavior patterns and platform dynamics.

Tools & Topics: Conceptual Modeling 路 Sense-Making Theory 路 Social Capital Theory 路 Online Moderation 路 Digital Trust 路 Misinformation Studies 路 Lucidchart

Key Contributions: Created an interactive diagram using Lucidchart to visualize relationships among trust, anonymity, moderation, and misinformation. Evaluated policy and design implications for online platforms, including the trade-offs between anonymity and accountability. Suggested improvements in AI-based moderation and trust-enhancing mechanisms like user-driven verification systems and transparent policies.
